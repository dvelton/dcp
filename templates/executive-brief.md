DCP — DOCUMENT CONTEXT PROTOCOL

Document Type:    Executive Brief
Audience:         C-suite, VP-level leadership, board members
Confidentiality:  [Specify: Internal / Confidential / Board-level]

Review Checklist:
□ Total length does not exceed two pages (one page preferred)
□ Lead paragraph states the decision or action needed — not background
□ Recommendation is clearly identified and separated from analysis
□ Risk is quantified or categorized, not described in vague terms
□ No unexplained legal jargon — every technical term is defined in context
□ Options are presented with clear tradeoffs, not just pros/cons
□ Timeline and next steps are specific (who, what, when)
□ Tone is appropriate for the audience — authoritative, not hedging

Drafting Standards:
- One page if at all possible; two pages maximum
- Open with the ask: what decision is needed, or what does leadership need to know
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences) and headers for scanability
- Use tables for comparisons and options — executives scan, they don't read linearly
- Bold key numbers, dates, and conclusions
- No throat-clearing introductions — get to the point immediately
- Write in active voice; attribute actions and decisions to specific people or teams
- If the answer is uncertain, say so directly and explain what would resolve the uncertainty

Constraints:
- Do not include detailed legal analysis — that belongs in the supporting memo
- Do not use footnotes or endnotes
- Do not hedge without offering a path to resolution

                  embedded policy is current if a policy server is available.
