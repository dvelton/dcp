DCP — DOCUMENT CONTEXT PROTOCOL

Document Type:    Data Processing Agreement
Audience:         Privacy counsel, counterparty DPO, procurement
Jurisdiction:     EU (GDPR) — adapt for other jurisdictions as applicable
Confidentiality:  Confidential

Review Checklist:
□ Verify controller/processor roles are correctly assigned — confirm which
   party determines purposes and means of processing
□ Check that subject matter, duration, nature, and purpose of processing
   are specifically described (Article 28(3) GDPR requirement)
□ Verify categories of data subjects and types of personal data are enumerated
□ Confirm processor obligations include: (i) process only on documented
   instructions, (ii) ensure personnel confidentiality, (iii) implement
   appropriate technical and organizational measures, (iv) assist with
   data subject rights, (v) assist with DPIA obligations
□ Review sub-processor provisions: prior written authorization required,
   flow-down of obligations, notification of changes with objection right
□ Check data breach notification timeline — must not exceed 72 hours for
   GDPR; confirm notification includes required details
□ Verify data return/deletion obligations upon termination — confirm
   timeline and certification requirements
□ Check audit rights — controller must have right to audit or inspect,
   either directly or through an independent auditor
□ Review cross-border transfer mechanisms — confirm adequacy decision,
   SCCs, or other valid transfer mechanism is specified
□ Confirm technical and organizational security measures are described
   with sufficient specificity (not just "industry standard")
□ Verify DPA survives termination of the main agreement for as long as
   processor retains personal data
□ Flag any limitation of liability specific to the DPA — should align
   with or be carved out from the main agreement's liability provisions

Drafting Standards:
- Formal regulatory tone — this document may be reviewed by supervisory authorities
- Reference specific GDPR articles where applicable
- Use defined terms consistently with the GDPR (controller, processor,
  personal data, data subject, processing)
- Structure follows Article 28(3) requirements
- Include annexes for: (i) description of processing, (ii) technical and
  organizational measures, (iii) list of approved sub-processors
