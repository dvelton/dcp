DCP — DOCUMENT CONTEXT PROTOCOL

Document Type:    Legal Memorandum
Audience:         Internal stakeholders, legal team, business partners
Confidentiality:  Attorney-client privileged / Work product

Review Checklist:
□ Issue statement is framed as a specific, answerable question
□ Relevant facts are complete and sourced — no unsupported assumptions
□ Analysis addresses both sides of the issue with strongest counterarguments
□ Risk assessment uses clear categories (high / medium / low) with reasoning
□ Recommendations are specific, actionable, and tied to the analysis
□ All options are presented with tradeoffs — not just the recommended path
□ Legal citations are accurate and current
□ Privilege header is present if applicable

Drafting Standards:
- Direct, analytical tone — lead with the conclusion, then explain
- Use the structure: Issue, Background, Analysis, Risk Assessment, Recommendation
- Frame advice in terms of risk and options, not abstract legal theory
- Write for a smart businessperson, not a law review — minimize jargon
- When legal terms are necessary, define them in plain language
- Keep paragraphs short — prefer 3-4 sentences maximum
- Use numbered lists for options and bullet points for supporting detail
- Bold key conclusions and risk ratings for scanability

                  embedded policy is current if a policy server is available.
