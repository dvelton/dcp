DCP — DOCUMENT CONTEXT PROTOCOL

Document Type:    RFP Response
Audience:         Prospective client evaluation committee, procurement team
Confidentiality:  Confidential — shared under NDA

Review Checklist:
□ Every question in the RFP is answered — no gaps or "N/A" without
   explanation
□ Responses directly address what was asked — no filler or marketing
   language that avoids the question
□ Technical capabilities are described accurately — do not overstate
   current functionality or conflate roadmap items with shipping features
□ Pricing is complete and consistent with the pricing model — no hidden
   fees or ambiguous terms
□ Client references are current and relevant to the prospect's industry
□ Compliance certifications cited are current — verify expiration dates
□ Implementation timeline is realistic and aligns with resource availability
□ Subcontractor or third-party dependencies are disclosed
□ Terms and conditions section does not inadvertently accept unfavorable
   terms from the RFP — flag any terms that conflict with standard positions
□ Executive summary leads with the prospect's problem, not our company history

Drafting Standards:
- Professional, confident tone — not salesy or superlative
- Lead every response with the direct answer, then provide supporting detail
- Use the prospect's terminology and language — mirror how they describe
  their needs, not how we describe our product
- Quantify claims wherever possible: "99.9% uptime SLA" not "highly reliable"
- Keep responses concise — evaluation committees review dozens of responses
- Use tables and structured formats for technical specifications
- Clearly distinguish between current capabilities, planned features
  (with expected dates), and partner/third-party capabilities
- Do not use "world-class," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge," or similar
  unsupported superlatives

Constraints:
- Do not commit to capabilities that are not generally available
- Do not accept or agree to RFP terms in the response — note that terms
  are subject to mutual agreement
- Do not disclose proprietary technical architecture details beyond what
  is necessary to answer the question
